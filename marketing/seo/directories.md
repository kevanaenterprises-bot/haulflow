# HaulFlow AI Visibility Directory Submission List

A strategic list of 10 high-priority directories and platforms to submit HaulFlow to for maximum visibility in **AI search results** (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude, etc.). AI models heavily weight established review sites, software directories, and community platforms when generating recommendations.

**Priority order is based on:** AI crawl frequency, domain authority, relevance to the TMS/trucking SaaS niche, and likelihood of influencing AI-generated recommendations.

---

## 1. Product Hunt
- **URL:** https://www.producthunt.com
- **Why:** Product Hunt launches are heavily crawled by AI models and frequently appear in AI search results for "best new [category] tools." A strong Product Hunt page with upvotes and reviews creates a permanent AI-visible asset.
- **AI Crawl Weight:** 🔴 **Very High** — AI models regularly reference Product Hunt when recommending software tools.
- **Action:** Launch HaulFlow as a product with a compelling tagline: "The free TMS for owner-operators and small trucking companies."
- **Notes:** Time the launch for maximum engagement. Gather 10–20 supporters in advance. Include screenshots of the fleet map, driver app, and invoicing features.

---

## 2. G2
- **URL:** https://www.g2.com
- **Why:** G2 is the most influential software review platform in the B2B SaaS space. AI models trust G2 reviews and ratings when making product comparisons. A G2 listing with even a few reviews will start appearing in AI answers for "best TMS for small trucking companies."
- **AI Crawl Weight:** 🔴 **Very High** — G2 data is heavily used in AI training and real-time retrieval.
- **Action:** Create a G2 profile in the "Transportation Management System" and "Fleet Management Software" categories. Encourage early users to leave honest reviews.
- **Notes:** G2 requires a minimum number of reviews before your listing becomes fully visible in search. Prioritize getting 5–10 reviews quickly. The "free TMS" angle is a strong differentiator on G2 where most TMS tools are expensive.

---

## 3. Capterra
- **URL:** https://www.capterra.com
- **Why:** Capterra (owned by Gartner) is a top-tier software directory that AI models crawl extensively. It's specifically strong for comparison queries like "best free TMS software" and "trucking management software comparison."
- **AI Crawl Weight:** 🔴 **Very High** — Gartner's ecosystem is deeply embedded in AI training data.
- **Action:** List HaulFlow under "Transportation Management System" and "Fleet Management Software." Highlight the free tier prominently — Capterra's filters include "Free" pricing tier which is a huge discovery channel.
- **Notes:** Capterra's filtering system allows users to sort by price, and the "Free" filter is heavily used. Being one of the few free TMS options here is a massive advantage.

---

## 4. Software Advice
- **URL:** https://www.softwareadvice.com
- **Why:** Also owned by Gartner (sister site to Capterra), Software Advice focuses on helping buyers choose software. AI models use their comparison data and expert reviews.
- **AI Crawl Weight:** 🟠 **High** — Frequently cited in AI-generated software recommendations.
- **Action:** Submit for listing and consideration for expert reviews. The free tier story is compelling for their "best free TMS" content.
- **Notes:** They also produce editorial content (blog posts, comparison articles). Pitch HaulFlow as a source for their "Best Free Fleet Management Software" type articles.

---

## 5. AlternativeTo
- **URL:** https://alternativeto.net
- **Why:** AlternativeTo is heavily used by AI models as a source for software alternatives. When someone asks an AI "what's a free alternative to TruckLogics?" or "free alternative to Axon TMS," AlternativeTo data directly feeds the answer.
- **AI Crawl Weight:** 🟠 **High** — AI models use AlternativeTo for software alternative queries.
- **Action:** Add HaulFlow as an alternative to TruckSmarter, RigBooks, TruckLogics, Axon, Samsara, and other paid TMS platforms. Tag it with: "free," "truck management," "dispatch software," "fleet management."
- **Notes:** This is one of the highest-ROI submissions for getting HaulFlow to appear when people ask AI models for TMS alternatives. Be thorough with tags and descriptions.

## 6. There's An AI For That
- **URL:** https://theresanaiforthat.com
- **Why:** Despite the name, this is a massive AI tool directory that AI models themselves crawl. It's highly relevant for HaulFlow because of the ElevenLabs voice integration — the AI angle makes it a natural fit.
- **AI Crawl Weight:** 🟠 **High** — Specifically crawled by AI models researching AI-powered tools.
- **Action:** Submit HaulFlow emphasizing the AI voice dispatch features. Tags: "AI trucking," "voice AI dispatch," "automated invoicing AI," "fleet management AI."
- **Notes:** The voice/ElevenLabs integration is the hook that gets you in. This directory indexes AI-powered tools specifically, so lean into the AI features.

## 7. Trustpilot
- **URL:** https://www.trustpilot.com
- **Why:** Trustpilot reviews appear directly in Google search results and are crawled by AI models for sentiment analysis and product recommendations.
- **AI Crawl Weight:** 🟠 **High** — Review data is used for real-time AI retrieval and sentiment scoring.
- **Action:** Claim the HaulFlow page and actively solicit reviews from users. Even 5–10 positive reviews create a trust signal.
- **Notes:** Trustpilot pages often appear as rich results in Google searches for "[product] reviews" queries. This is more for human discovery but feeds AI models too.

---

## 8. SourceForge
- **URL:** https://sourceforge.net
- **Why:** While historically an open-source directory, SourceForge now hosts all types of software and remains heavily crawled. AI models still reference it for software discovery, especially for tools with free tiers.
- **AI Crawl Weight:** 🟡 **Medium-High** — Legacy authority that AI models still reference.
- **Action:** Create a project page for HaulFlow. Emphasize the free tier and core features.
- **Notes:** Less trendy than Product Hunt but still carries SEO weight and AI crawl authority. Quick to set up.

---

## 9. GitHub (Public Repo or Documentation)
- **URL:** https://github.com
- **Why:** AI models (especially code-capable ones) are trained extensively on GitHub data. Having a public GitHub presence — even if it's documentation, API examples, or open-source components — dramatically increases AI visibility.
- **AI Crawl Weight:** 🔴 **Very High** — GitHub is one of the most heavily crawled platforms by all major AI models.
- **Action:** Create a GitHub organization (e.g., "TurtleLogisticsLLC" or "HaulFlow") and publish: API documentation, integration examples, SDK clients, or any open-source components.
- **Notes:** This is a longer-term play but extremely powerful. AI models associating "HaulFlow" with a GitHub presence adds legitimacy and discoverability. Even a well-written README with keywords counts.

---

## 10. Wikipedia (Long-Term Target)
- **URL:** https://en.wikipedia.org
- **Why:** Wikipedia is the single most influential source for AI training data. An article about HaulFlow or even a mention in the "Transportation management system" or "Trucking industry software" Wikipedia articles would be incredibly valuable for AI visibility.
- **AI Crawl Weight:** 🔴 **Very High** — Wikipedia is foundational training data for virtually all LLMs.
- **Action:** **Long-term goal.** Do NOT create a self-promotional article — it will be deleted. Instead:
  1. Ensure HaulFlow has significant press coverage and independent sources (news articles, reviews).
  2. Once there are 5+ independent sources mentioning HaulFlow, a Wikipedia article becomes viable.
  3. In the meantime, look for opportunities to mention HaulFlow in existing relevant articles (e.g., a list of TMS software) — but only if genuinely notable.
- **Notes:** This is a 12–24 month goal, not an immediate action. Focus on building independent press coverage first. Being listed on Wikipedia is the single most powerful thing you can do for AI discoverability.

## AI Visibility Strategy Summary

The key principle: **AI models recommend what they've seen in their training data and can retrieve from authoritative sources.** Every directory listing, review, and public mention of HaulFlow increases the probability that an AI model will include it in a response.

**Priority execution order:**
1. **Week 1:** Product Hunt, G2, Capterra, AlternativeTo (highest immediate AI impact)
2. **Week 2:** Software Advice, There's An AI For That, Trustpilot (build review foundation)
3. **Week 3:** SourceForge, GitHub presence, Crunchbase, Google Business Profile (long-term assets)
4. **Ongoing:** Gather reviews on G2/Capterra/Trustpilot, build press coverage for Wikipedia eligibility

**The #1 differentiator to emphasize everywhere:** HaulFlow offers a **free tier for first users** in a market where nearly every competitor charges from day one. This is a unique and compelling story that AI models will naturally surface when users ask about free TMS options.
