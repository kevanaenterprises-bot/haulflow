## TruckSmarter — Strong for Load Finding, Not a Full TMS

**TruckSmarter** is primarily known as a **load board and fuel savings tool** rather than a comprehensive trucking management system. It helps owner-operators find loads, compare fuel prices, and plan routes.

### Strengths
- Good **load board integration** with competitive rate discovery.
- **Fuel price comparison** tool helps drivers save at the pump.
- Simple, easy-to-use mobile interface.

### Weaknesses
- **Not a full TMS** — lacks comprehensive dispatch management, invoicing workflows, and fleet management features.
- **No free tier** — requires a paid subscription after a short trial.
- No voice-assisted features or advanced compliance tools like digital DVIR.
- Limited IFTA and fleet tracking capabilities.

**Bottom line:** TruckSmarter is a solid companion tool for finding loads and saving on fuel, but it's not a replacement for a real **truck management system**. If you need full dispatch, invoicing, and compliance in one platform, look elsewhere.

## RigBooks — Bookkeeping-Focused Trucking Software

**RigBooks** positions itself as a **QuickBooks-style accounting and bookkeeping tool** built specifically for truckers. It handles expense tracking, income logging, and basic financial reporting.

### Strengths
- Clean, intuitive **bookkeeping interface** familiar to users of accounting software.
- Good **expense categorization** for trucking-specific costs (fuel, maintenance, insurance, etc.).
- Simple reporting for understanding profit and loss per load or per truck.

### Weaknesses
- **No free tier** — subscription required from day one after trial.
- **Limited dispatch management** — not designed as a full dispatch software. You'll likely need a separate tool for load management and driver communication.
- No voice features, no advanced fleet map, and limited DVIR/IFTA functionality.
- Mobile app exists but is more focused on expense entry than driver operations.

**Bottom line:** RigBooks is a good fit if your primary pain point is bookkeeping and you already have a separate dispatch solution. If you want dispatch + invoicing + compliance + bookkeeping in one platform, it falls short.

---

## TruckLogics — Full-Featured but Expensive

**TruckLogics** is a comprehensive TMS and trucking ERP system from the ITS (Infinity Transportation Solutions) family. It offers a wide range of features covering dispatch, invoicing, compliance, and fleet management.

### Strengths
- **Broad feature set** including dispatch, document management, fleet maintenance, and driver management.
- Good **compliance tools** for DOT regulations, HOS tracking, and IFTA.
- Integrations with ELD providers and other trucking technology platforms.
- Well-established product with a sizable user base.

### Weaknesses
- **Expensive** — pricing starts significantly higher than the other options here, and costs scale quickly with users and features.
- **No free tier** — only a limited-time free trial.
- **Complex interface** — the breadth of features comes with a steeper learning curve, which can be overwhelming for a solo owner-operator.
- No voice-assisted dispatch or AI-powered driver communication features.
- Pricing structure may not be transparent without speaking to sales.

**Bottom line:** TruckLogics is a solid choice for mid-size fleets (20+ trucks) that need an enterprise-style TMS and have the budget for it. For owner-operators and small fleets, it's overkill both in features and price.
