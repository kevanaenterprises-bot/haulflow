Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 23, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains what information HaulFlow collects, why we collect it, how we use and protect it, and the choices you have. It applies to the HaulFlow website, web application, and mobile apps (together, the “Service”).

HaulFlow is fleet and load-management software for trucking companies and owner-operators. The Service is operated by GO Farms & Cattle LLC (“HaulFlow,” “we,” “us,” or “our”). By using the Service, you agree to the practices described here.

1. Information We Collect

Information you provide

  • Account & company details: your name, business name, email address, phone number, and login credentials.
  • Driver records you enter: driver names, phone numbers, email addresses, CDL (commercial driver’s license) numbers, license and medical-card expiration dates, and documents you upload such as CDL files and medical cards.
  • Customer and shipper details you add to manage loads, including contact and address information.
  • Load and dispatch data: pickup and delivery locations, status, and related notes you create in the Service.
  • Payment information: when you subscribe, billing is processed by our payment provider, Stripe. We do not store your full card number on our systems.
  • Support communications: messages you send us and information you include in them.

Information collected automatically

  • Usage and device data: IP address, browser or device type, app version, pages or screens viewed, and timestamps, used to operate and secure the Service.
  • Cookies and similar technologies used to keep you signed in and remember preferences.

Location information (mobile app)

With the driver’s permission, the HaulFlow mobile app collects the driver’s precise device location, including while the app runs in the background or is closed. Background location is essential to the core purpose of the app and is used to:

  • Calculate IFTA mileage — recording miles driven in each state so fuel and mileage taxes can be reported accurately;
  • Verify arrivals and departures — using geofences to log timestamped entry and exit at shippers and receivers, which helps resolve detention-pay disputes; and
  • Track loads in transit — showing a vehicle’s location to authorized dispatchers while a load is active.

Location is associated with the driver’s phone number and the specific load being carried. We use the phone number only to link location to the correct driver and load; we do not collect the driver’s name or other personal profile information as part of location tracking. We do not sell location data or use it for advertising. A driver can disable location access at any time in device settings, though doing so will prevent IFTA mileage, geofence timestamps, and load tracking from working.

3. How We Share Information

We do not sell your personal information. We share it only as needed to run the Service:

  • Service providers who operate on our behalf, such as Stripe (payments) and our cloud hosting and database providers. They may access information only to perform services for us and are bound to protect it.
  • Within your organization: information you enter is available to authorized users of your company’s account, including drivers who log in to the driver portal.
  • Legal and safety reasons: when required by law, regulation, or legal process, or to protect the rights, property, or safety of HaulFlow, our users, or others.
  • Business transfers: in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, subject to this Policy.

4. Your Business Data Belongs to You

The information you enter into HaulFlow — including your customers, rates, loads, and other business records — belongs to you, not to us. We do not access, view, use, or disclose your business data for our own purposes.

We access the contents of your account only when you ask us to — for example, when you request support or report a problem and grant us access so we can investigate. Outside of those situations, and except where strictly necessary to operate, secure, or back up the Service, or where required by law, your business information remains private to your organization. We will never sell it, and we will never share it with other customers or third parties for marketing.

5. Data Retention

6. Security

We use reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect information, including encrypted connections and protected credentials. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, so we cannot guarantee absolute security.

7. Your Choices and Rights

  • Deletion: you may request that we delete your account and associated personal information, subject to legal retention requirements.
  • Communications: you can opt out of non-essential emails using the unsubscribe link or by contacting us.

Depending on where you live, you may have additional rights under applicable privacy laws. Contact us to exercise any of these rights.

8. Children’s Privacy

The Service is intended for businesses and users who are at least 18 years old. It is not directed to children, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided us information, contact us and we will delete it.

9. International Users
